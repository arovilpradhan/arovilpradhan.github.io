In my previous role at Hitachi Vantara, I provided L1–L2 IT support to internal users through ServiceNow, using remote tools like Bomgar, MS Teams, and phone, while also offering onsite assistance when needed to ensure SLA compliance. I managed laptop and accessory inventory with complete accuracy, coordinated vendor service requests, and supported both routine and yearly IT hardware audits for the Pune site. I handled secure user onboarding and offboarding, provisioning assets, ensuring security compliance, and coordinating asset recovery. I collaborated with cross-functional teams—including Network, Infrastructure, and Engineering—to troubleshoot and escalate critical issues, and actively participated in scheduled IT maintenance windows. My responsibilities also included reviewing vulnerability reports in Qualys (read-only access), remediating flagged devices through software removal or OS reinstallation, validating fixes, and reporting them for compliance closure. I supported AV and video conferencing setups across locations, handled warranty cases for Lenovo and Apple devices, contributed to IT outage resolution, and conducted internal knowledge-sharing sessions to upskill IT and cross-functional teams.

During my early days at Hitachi Vantara, I was involved in resolving a global IT outage caused by a misconfiguration in the Carbon Black endpoint security tool, which blocked many users worldwide from logging into their systems. With limited guidance and mounting pressure, I devised a manual recovery method by leveraging BitLocker recovery in Windows recovery mode. This involved gaining administrative command-line access by replacing sethc.exe with cmd.exe, resetting the local admin account password, and disabling Carbon Black services through the registry. I successfully used this method to recover several user machines, and my approach was immediately shared by my manager on a company-wide desktop operations call across multiple sites. A few days later, I learned that my workaround was later scripted and deployed enterprise-wide by the desktop engineering and security teams to automate the recovery process. Contributing to a live enterprise incident at such an early stage helped sharpen my problem-solving mindset and reinforced the importance of creative thinking in high-pressure situations.
